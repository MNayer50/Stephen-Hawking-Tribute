Stephen Hawking in 1979

    Timeline of Stephen Hawking's life

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England, United Kingdom on the 300th anniversary of Galileo's death
  • 1959 - Hawking began his university education at University College, Oxford
  • 1962 - Began graduate work at Trinity Hall, Cambridge
  • 1963 -Diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis and given two and a half years to live
  • 1965 - Marries Jane Wilde, his first wife
  • 1966 - Gained his PhD with his thesis Properties of Expanding Universes
  • 1970 - Working with Roger Penrose, he showed that Black Holes can emit radiation
  • 1974 - Published his paper entitled "Black hole explosions?" which showed that due to quantum effects, Hawking radiation is released by black holes, causing them to evaporate over time.
  • 1977 - Appointed professor of gravitational physics at Cambridge
  • 1979 - Appointed Lucasian professor of mathematics at Cambridge which is a chair founded by in 1663 with money left in will of Henry Lucas, who was a member of parliment for the University. The chair was one held by Issac Newton from 1669 to 1702
  • 1985 - Lost what remained of his speech in a life-threatening bout of pneumonia
  • 2005 - Argued in a 2005 paper that the information paradox was explained by examining all the alternative histories of universes, with the information loss in those with black holes being cancelled out by those without such loss.
  • 2009 - Held a party open to all, but publicised the party only after it was over, so that only time-travellers could attend. No one showed up.
  • 2015 - Helped launch Breakthrough Initiatives, an effort to search for Extraterrestrial life
  • 2018 - Published his final paper A smooth exit from eternal inflation?
  • 2018 - Died on March 14, in his home in Cambridge, England on the 139th anniversary of Einstein's birth. His ashes were buried in the Scientist's Corner in Westminster Abbey between the graves of Charles Darwin and Isaac Newton.

Stephen Hawking also received many awards during his life including:

Adams Prize (1966), Eddington Medal (1975), Institute of Physics James Clerk Maxwell Medal and Prize (1976), Albert Einstein Medal (1979), Commander of the Order of the British Empire (1982), Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society (1985), Dirac Medal of the Institute of Physics (1987), Wolf Prize in Physics (1988), Companion of Honour (1989), Copley Medal (2006), Fonseca Prize (2008), Presidential Medal of Freedom (2009), Special Prize in Fundamental Physics (2013)

Stephen Hawking authored many books that sought to answer fundamental questions about the universe including:

The Formation and Evolution of Cosmic Strings (1990), Black Holes and Baby Universes (1993), The Nature of Space and Time (1996), The Universe in a Nutshell (2001), The Theory of Everything: The Origin and Fate of the Universe (2002), On the Shoulders of Giants: The Great Works of Physics and Astronomy (2002), A Briefer History of Time (2005), God Created the Integers: The Mathematical Breakthroughs That Changed History (2005), The Grand Design (2010), My Brief History (2013), Brief Answers to the Big Questions (2018)

He also held several public lectures:

  • Life in the Universe, 1996
  • The Beginning of Time, 1996
  • Does God play Dice?, 1999
  • Space and Time Warps, 1999
  • Godel and the End of the Universe, 2002
  • The Origin of the Universe, 2005
  • Into a Black Hole, 2008
